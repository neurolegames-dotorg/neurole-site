The neuroscience game built for real learning

Neurole is a free daily neuroscience game designed for pre-med students, neuroscience majors, medical students, clinicians, and anyone curious about how the brain works. Think of it as Wordle for neurology — a new clinical case every day where you guess the neurological diagnosis from five symptoms.

Our second game, Map the Brain, is a free neuroanatomy quiz where real brain images appear and you identify the highlighted region from four choices. After answering, an AI tutor explains the region's function, clinical relevance, and connections to other areas. It's the fastest way to memorise the difference between Wernicke's area and Broca's area, or understand why damage to the hippocampus causes anterograde amnesia.

Neurole covers sulci, gyri, cortical areas, and neuropsychology — from the prefrontal cortex to the periaqueductal gray. All cases are reviewed for clinical accuracy. All content is free, forever.
