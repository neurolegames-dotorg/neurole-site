Neuropsychology — Key Areas

Brain regions critical for understanding psychological and behavioural processes.

Prefrontal Cortex

Executive function, decision-making, impulse control, and working memory. Implicated in ADHD, depression, and schizophrenia.

Amygdala

Fear, threat detection, and emotional memory. Central to anxiety disorders, PTSD, and phobias.

Hippocampus

Memory formation and spatial navigation. Severely affected in Alzheimer's and major depression.

Anterior Cingulate Cortex

Error detection, conflict monitoring, and emotional regulation. Involved in OCD and mood disorders.

Insula

Interoception, pain, and emotional awareness. Plays a role in addiction and eating disorders.

Ventral Striatum

Reward anticipation and motivation. A core structure in addiction, depression, and anhedonia.

Orbitofrontal Cortex

Evaluates reward value and consequence. Disrupted in OCD, impulse-control disorders, and substance use.

Hypothalamus

Regulates stress hormones (via HPA axis), sleep, and appetite. Central to mood and anxiety disorders.
